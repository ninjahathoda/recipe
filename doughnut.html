Doughnuts Recipes

Glazed Doughnut

Glazed doughnuts are a timeless classic, loved for their simplicity and irresistible sweetness. Here's a bit more detail about them:

Ingredients:

  • 2 and 1/4 teaspoons (1 packet) active dry yeast
  • 3/4 cup warm milk (around 110°F or 45°C)
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1/4 cup milk or water
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast in the warm milk and let it sit for about 5 minutes until foamy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the sugar, egg, melted butter, and vanilla extract. Mix well.
  3. Add the yeast mixture to the wet ingredients and mix until combined.
  4. Gradually add the flour and salt to the wet ingredients, stirring until a dough forms.
  5. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  6. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until doubled in size.
  7. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and roll it out on a floured surface to about 1/2 inch thickness.

Boston Cream Doughnuts

Boston cream doughnuts are a delightful treat that combines the best elements of doughnuts and pastry cream. Here's a recipe to make them at home:

Ingredients:

  • 2 and 1/4 teaspoons (1 packet) active dry yeast
  • 3/4 cup warm milk (around 110°F or 45°C)
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Instructions:

  1. Follow steps 1-6 from the glazed doughnut recipe to prepare the dough and allow it to rise.
  2. After the dough has risen, punch it down and roll it out on a floured surface to about 1/2 inch thickness.
  3. Use a doughnut cutter or a round cutter to cut out doughnuts. Place the doughnuts on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, cover them with a clean kitchen towel, and let them rise for another 30-45 minutes.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or heavy-bottomed pot to 350°F (175°C).
  5. Carefully place the doughnuts in the hot oil, frying them for about 1-2 minutes on each side until golden brown.
  6. Remove the doughnuts from the oil using a slotted spoon and place them on a wire rack to drain and cool.

Apple Fritters

Apple fritters are a delightful treat, especially when served warm with a dusting of powdered sugar or a drizzle of glaze

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cups apples, peeled, cored, and diced (such as Granny Smith or Honeycrisp)
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, and ground cinnamon.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, and melted butter until well combined.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
  4. Gently fold in the diced apples until evenly distributed throughout the batter.
  5. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or heavy-bottomed pot to 350°F (175°C).
  6. Using a spoon or cookie scoop, carefully drop spoonfuls of batter into the hot oil, frying a few fritters at a time. Be sure not to overcrowd the pot.
  7. Fry the fritters for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and cooked through.
  8. Use a slotted spoon to transfer the cooked fritters to a wire rack lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.

Crullers

Crullers are a classic pastry known for their twisted shape and light, airy texture. They're typically deep-fried and can be glazed or dusted with sugar.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 large eggs
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2-3 tablespoons milk or water
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine water, butter, sugar, and salt. Bring to a boil over medium heat.
  2. Once the mixture is boiling, remove the saucepan from heat and add the flour all at once. Stir vigorously until the mixture forms a ball of dough and pulls away from the sides of the pan.
  3. Transfer the dough to a mixing bowl and let it cool for about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition, until the dough is smooth and glossy.
  5. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or heavy-bottomed pot to 375°F (190°C).
  6. Fill a pastry bag fitted with a star tip with the dough.
  7. Pipe the dough into 3-4 inch strips directly into the hot oil, using kitchen scissors to cut the dough from the pastry bag. You can also pipe the dough into circles or twists, if desired.
  8. Fry the crullers for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and cooked through.
  9. Use a slotted spoon to transfer the cooked crullers to a wire rack lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.
