Waffles Recipes

Classic Waffles

These classic waffles are light, fluffy, and perfect for a weekend breakfast. Serve them with butter and maple syrup for a delightful treat!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 3/4 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, beat eggs; stir in milk, oil, and vanilla extract.
  3. Stir into dry ingredients just until combined.
  4. Bake in a preheated waffle iron according to manufacturer's directions until golden brown.
  5. Serve hot with your favorite toppings.

Belgian Waffles

These Belgian waffles are crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. They're perfect for a special weekend brunch with your favorite toppings!

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 1/4 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, sift together the flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar.
  2. In another bowl, whisk together the milk, oil, eggs, and vanilla extract.
  3. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  4. Cook in a preheated waffle iron until golden and crisp.
  5. Serve hot with your favorite toppings.

Liege Waffles

Liege waffles are a delightful treat with their caramelized exterior and soft, sweet interior. Here's a recipe to make them at home:

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cups (280g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 package (2 1/4 teaspoons) active dry yeast
  • 1/3 cup (80ml) warm milk
  • 2 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup (200g) pearl sugar
  • 1 cup (2 sticks or 226g) unsalted butter, softened

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast in the warm milk and let it sit for about 5 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the eggs, vanilla extract, and yeast mixture.
  3. Stir the ingredients together until a dough forms. The dough will be sticky.
  4. Add the softened butter to the dough, mixing until it's fully incorporated and the dough becomes smooth and elastic. This may take some effort, but keep mixing until the butter is well combined.
  5. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let the dough rise in a warm place for about 1 to 2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.
  6. Once the dough has risen, gently fold in the pearl sugar until evenly distributed throughout the dough.
  7. Preheat your waffle iron according to its manufacturer's instructions.
  8. Divide the dough into small balls, about the size of a golf ball.
  9. Place a ball of dough onto the preheated waffle iron and cook according to the manufacturer's instructions, usually for about 3-5 minutes, or until the waffle is golden brown and crispy.
  10. Remove the waffles from the iron and let them cool slightly before serving. Optionally, sprinkle some additional pearl sugar on top for extra sweetness.
  11. Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls until all the waffles are cooked.
  12. Serve the Liege waffles warm and enjoy their delicious caramelized goodness!

Classic American Waffles

Classic American waffles are a breakfast staple, loved for their crisp exterior and fluffy interior. Here's a recipe to make them at home:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 3/4 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your waffle iron according to its manufacturer's instructions.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, beat the eggs. Then add the milk, melted butter, and vanilla extract. Mix until well combined.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix; it's okay if there are a few lumps.
  5. Ladle the batter onto the preheated waffle iron, spreading it out evenly. Close the lid and cook according to the waffle iron's instructions, usually for about 3-5 minutes, or until the waffles are golden brown and crispy.
  6. Carefully remove the waffles from the iron and serve immediately, or keep them warm in a low oven while you cook the remaining batter.
  7. Serve the waffles with your favorite toppings such as maple syrup, fresh fruit, whipped cream, or even a dusting of powdered sugar.

Hong Kong Style Waffles

Hong Kong Style Waffles, also known as Egg Waffles or Bubble Waffles, are a popular street food snack in Hong Kong. They're characterized by their unique spherical egg-shaped cells and have a crispy exterior with a soft, chewy interior. Here's a recipe to make them at home:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon custard powder (optional, for added flavor and color)
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your Hong Kong egg waffle maker according to the manufacturer's instructions.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, custard powder (if using), baking powder, cornstarch, sugar, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, melted butter, and vanilla extract until well combined.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and whisk until you have a smooth batter. Let the batter rest for about 10-15 minutes to allow the ingredients to hydrate and the bubbles to settle.
  5. Lightly grease the egg molds of the waffle maker with oil or non-stick cooking spray.
  6. Pour the batter into the molds, filling them about 3/4 full. Be careful not to overfill, as the batter will expand as it cooks.
  7. Close the waffle maker and cook according to the manufacturer's instructions, usually for about 3-5 minutes, or until the waffles are golden brown and crispy.
  8. Carefully remove the waffles from the molds using a fork or chopsticks, and let them cool slightly on a wire rack to maintain their crispiness.
  9. Repeat the process with the remaining batter until all the waffles are cooked.
  10. Serve the Hong Kong Style Waffles warm and enjoy them plain or with your favorite toppings, such as ice cream, fresh fruit, chocolate sauce, or whipped cream.
  11. Store any leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to a day. Reheat in a toaster oven or oven to restore crispiness before serving.
