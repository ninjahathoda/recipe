Hard Candies

Making hard candies at home is a fun and rewarding process. Here's a basic recipe for making homemade hard candies:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup light corn syrup
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Food coloring (optional)
  • Flavoring extract (such as peppermint, cinnamon, lemon, etc.)
  • Confectioners' sugar or cornstarch, for dusting (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Grease candy molds or a baking sheet with cooking spray or butter. If using molds, place lollipop sticks in the molds if making lollipops.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the flavoring extract and food coloring (if using) and set aside.
  3. Have the candy thermometer ready and ensure it's calibrated properly
  4. In a heavy-bottomed saucepan, combine the granulated sugar, corn syrup, and water. Stir until the sugar is dissolved.
  5. Place the saucepan over medium-high heat and insert the candy thermometer.
  6. Cook the sugar syrup without stirring until it reaches the hard-crack stage, which is around 300째F (150째C). This can take about 15-20 minutes.
  7. Once the candies are completely cooled and hardened, carefully remove them from the molds or baking sheet.
  8. If desired, dust the candies with confectioners' sugar or cornstarch to prevent sticking.
  9. Store the homemade hard candies in an airtight container at room temperature. They should stay fresh for several weeks.

Sour Candies

Making sour candies at home can be a fun project, and you can customize the level of sourness to your preference.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 cup light corn syrup
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice (or other citrus juice)
  • 1-2 tablespoons citric acid (adjust to taste)
  • Food coloring (optional)
  • Sour sugar or citric acid for coating (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Grease candy molds or a baking sheet with cooking spray or butter. If using molds, place lollipop sticks in the molds if making lollipops.
  2. Have the candy thermometer ready and ensure it's calibrated properly.
  3. In a small bowl, mix together the citric acid with a little bit of granulated sugar. Set aside.
  4. In a heavy-bottomed saucepan, combine the granulated sugar, water, corn syrup, and lemon juice.
  5. Stir the mixture over medium heat until the sugar is dissolved.
  6. Once the sugar is dissolved, stop stirring and insert the candy thermometer into the saucepan.
  7. Cook the mixture until it reaches the hard-crack stage, which is around 300째F (150째C). This can take about 15-20 minutes.
  8. Once the candies are completely cooled and hardened, carefully remove them from the molds or baking sheet.
  9. Store the homemade sour candies in an airtight container at room temperature. They should stay fresh for several weeks.

Caramel Candies

Making homemade caramel candies is a delicious endeavor that results in rich, buttery treats.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 cup unsalted butter (2 sticks)
  • 1 cup light corn syrup
  • 1 can (14 ounces) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Optional: Sea salt for topping (if making salted caramel candies)

Instructions:

  1. Line a 9x9 inch (or similar size) baking pan with parchment paper, leaving some overhang on the sides for easy removal. Grease the parchment paper with butter or cooking spray.
  2. In a heavy-bottomed saucepan, combine the granulated sugar, brown sugar, butter, and corn syrup over medium heat. Stir until the butter is melted and the sugars are dissolved.
  3. If making salted caramel candies, sprinkle sea salt evenly over the surface of the hot caramel. Use as much or as little salt as you prefer.
  4. Let the caramel candies cool at room temperature for at least 4 hours, or until completely set and firm.
  5. Once the caramel is set, use the parchment paper overhang to lift the caramel block out of the pan.
  6. Place the block of caramel on a cutting board and use a sharp knife to cut it into individual pieces. You can cut them into squares, rectangles, or any desired shape.

Novelty Candies

Novelty candies are whimsical treats that often come in fun shapes, colors, and flavors, appealing to both children and adults alike.

Ingredients:

  • Assorted small candies (such as hard candies, gumdrops, jelly beans, or candy-coated chocolates)
  • Elastic string or thread
  • Optional: Large candy beads or charms for focal points

Instructions:

  1. Sort through your assortment of small candies and choose the ones you'd like to use for your candy necklace or bracelet. You can use a variety of colors, shapes, and flavors to make your creations more visually appealing.
  2. Cut a length of elastic string or thread to your desired length for the necklace or bracelet. Make sure it's long enough to fit comfortably around your neck or wrist with some extra length for tying.
  3. Thread the candies onto the string, alternating colors and shapes to create a visually appealing pattern. You can also add large candy beads or charms as focal points if desired.
  4. Once you've finished threading the candies onto the string, tie the ends securely together in a knot. Make sure the knot is tight enough to hold the candies in place but not too tight that it cuts into the candies.
  5. If needed, adjust the spacing of the candies on the string to ensure they're evenly distributed. You can also trim any excess string from the ends of the necklace or bracelet
  6. Your homemade candy necklace or bracelet is now ready to be enjoyed or given as a gift! Wear it as a fun accessory or share it with friends and family.

Chocolate

Making chocolates at home can be a delightful and rewarding experience. Here's a basic recipe for making homemade chocolates:

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces (about 225 grams) of chocolate (dark, milk, or white), finely chopped or in chip form
  • Optional: Fillings such as nuts, dried fruit, caramel, peanut butter, or flavored extracts

Instructions:

  1. Place the chopped chocolate in a heatproof bowl set over a pot of simmering water (double boiler method). Make sure the bowl doesn't touch the water.
  2. Stir the chocolate occasionally until it's completely melted and smooth. Alternatively, you can melt the chocolate in the microwave at 30-second intervals, stirring between each interval until melted.
  3. If you're using chocolate molds, ensure they are clean and dry. You can find various shapes and sizes of molds at baking supply stores or online.
  4. Use a pastry brush or spoon to coat the inside of the molds with melted chocolate, ensuring an even layer. Tap the molds gently on the counter to remove any air bubbles.
  5. If you're adding fillings to your chocolates, such as nuts or dried fruit, place a small amount into each mold cavity on top of the melted chocolate layer. Make sure not to overfill the molds.
  6. Place the filled molds in the refrigerator for about 15-20 minutes, or until the chocolates are set and firm.
  7. Transfer the chocolates to an airtight container and store them in a cool, dry place. They can also be stored in the refrigerator for longer shelf life, especially if your fillings are perishable.
